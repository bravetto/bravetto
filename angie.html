§IGlossary

Four words carry everything that follows.

Object
A thing that exists in its own right — with structure (attributes), instances (multiple real occurrences), and purpose (it matters to a person or a business). A clinical trial is an object. A position at a company is an object. A certification is an object.
Relationship
How two objects connect. A Person holds a Certification. A Person worked at an Organization. A Patient was referred to a Care Pathway.
Junction Object
A thing that exists only because two other things met. A position is the junction of a Person and an Organization across a Period. An appointment is the junction of a Patient and a Clinician at a moment in time. The junction is where the meaning lives.
Wrapper
A label that pretends to be an object but isn't. "Dashboard." "Profile." "Currently." "How To Reach Her." Wrappers fragment objects across screens and obscure the things that actually carry the work. The discipline of OOUX is the discipline of refusing them.
