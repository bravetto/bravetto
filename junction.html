There is a kind of map that has not yet been drawn. The org-chart shows authority. The workflow shows sequence. The system diagram shows components. None of these show the organism — the thing the organization actually is when you stop drawing it as a hierarchy and start drawing it as Objects in relation.

OOUX/ORCA gives us the vocabulary for the product layer: Objects, Relationships, Calls-to-Action, Attributes. This map proposes a single relocation and a single extension.

The relocation: apply the framework upstream — to the organization itself, before the product. The extension: name a new class — the Junction Object — for what emerges when two Objects fuse and produce a third with at least one property that exists in neither parent alone.

What follows is the first map drawn in this language. Its subject is Sophia Prater's own organism. Its purpose is to make visible what is currently held only in her head — and to offer back, with care, a class extension that may be useful.
