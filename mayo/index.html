01 · The opportunity

People with serious or complex care needs do not have a unified journey.

They arrive through search, AI answers, condition pages, referrals, and care pages. Each surface may be useful on its own. The decision journey across them is not.

Current pressure

Content is accurate but fragmented.

Mayo's expertise is real and the content is correct, but the path from a serious-care question to a confident next step is scattered across surfaces that don't carry context forward.

The opportunity

Make the journey navigable.

Care Hub connects condition understanding to Mayo's differentiated care model without collapsing the boundaries that keep syndicated content clean — and shows up the same way in search, AI answers, and Mayo-owned pages.

Why this matters now

  • Truth versus persuasion. Patients distrust marketing but still need evidence and confidence signals before taking action.
  • Canonical versus contextual. Generic condition truth and Mayo-specific expertise both matter — they need governed boundaries.
  • Promise versus operational reality. "We coordinate care for you" must be meaningful without overpromising physician-level booking or exposing system seams.

03 · Experience Layer

How object truth becomes useful in decision moments.

The Experience Layer sequences canonical objects into the moments where a patient, caregiver, or referring clinician is actually trying to orient, build confidence, or take action.

Find

Entry

The user is trying to orient: what is this care area, and is it relevant to me?

Likely surfaces: internal search, search engine entry, answer-engine result, topic hub.
Trust

Fit & Confidence

The user needs to understand whether Mayo has the expertise, care model, and evidence that justify the next step.

Likely surfaces: AI answer, care overview, evidence block, patient example, clinician support.
Act

Eligibility & Action

The user needs a clear path to request evaluation, understand logistics, and trust that Mayo will coordinate care.

Likely surfaces: Care Hub page, pathway module, request-evaluation CTA, support-service content.
04 · The bridge

Between source truth and decision moment is a layer that has not yet been defined.

OOUX gives the system its canonical objects. The Experience Layer gives users their decision moments. Both are working. The middle — the practice that turns canonical objects into the experiences those moments need — is yet to be defined. That definition is the work this engagement is here to do.

The middle layer

Naming and bridging the gap is the work.

The middle is not a deliverable type. It is the practice of turning stable object truth into moment-shaped experience without losing governance — and of letting human observation update source truth back. We are not here to declare what to call it. We are here to help define what it is, with you, until the practice can stand.

"OOUX and Experience Layer work together but need a third, middle layer that is yet to be defined — that we need the agency to help us bridge that gap. And what that gap is."

06 · Why it matters

A governed decision system is scalable, AI-ready, and trustable for engineering.

Better patient guidance

Decision moments become clearer across search, AI answers, and Mayo-owned pages — without inventing new content for each surface.

Governed content reuse

Core claims, evidence, and CTAs come from canonical objects rather than one-off page copy that drifts over time.

AI readiness

Answer engines can draw from structured, attributed content without blurring the syndicated-versus-Mayo boundary.

Engineering alignment

Objects, patterns, assembly rules, and rendered instances become contracts — not subjective design notes.
