DESIGN

Revenue Event + Failure Posture

The Revenue Event: Captured Booking

Success = caller provides name, day, time, phone, reason for visit
Abe confirms details and promises staff follow-up
Staff confirms the appointment — Abe never says "you're booked"
A caller ready to book must never hang up without data captured

When Something Breaks

Abe says: "I'm having a little trouble — can I get your name and number so someone can call you right back?"
Max latency: 3 seconds. Max turns: 10. Max silence retries: 2.
TTS failure → Polly robot voice (keep call alive)
LLM failure → pre-cached intent response
Worst case: Abe captures info and promises callback. Never dead air. Never confusion.
OBJECTIONS

If They Push Back

What if patients don't like talking to AI?
Most callers won't know. Abe sounds natural, uses 1-2 sentences, never says "I'm an AI." If someone asks directly, Abe is honest. The experience feels like calling a warm, helpful receptionist. The goal isn't to fool anyone — it's to help everyone.
What if it gives wrong medical information?
It can't. Abe is hardcoded to never diagnose, never give medical advice, never share patient data. If a caller asks a medical question, Abe says: "That's a great question for the doctor — let me get you scheduled." It deflects to booking every time.
We already have a phone system that works fine.
Does it answer at 6:15 PM? Does it answer during Saturday rush? Does it capture every booking request when staff is with patients? Every missed call is a missed appointment. Abe doesn't replace your phone system — it makes sure nobody falls through the cracks.
How do we know if it's actually working?
Every call is transcribed and summarized. Larry sees a daily report: how many calls, what they asked, how they felt, what was captured. More data in week one than you've had in the entire history of the practice.
Can we try it before we commit?
That's exactly what Phase 1 is. We turn it on. You watch it work. If you don't see value in two weeks, we turn it off. You risk nothing. The phone number is already live.
What happens if the technology fails mid-call?
Abe captures their name and number, says "someone from our team will call you right back," and ends the call cleanly. The worst thing that can happen is: a patient gets a callback. That's already better than voicemail.
