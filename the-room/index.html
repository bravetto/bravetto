Advanced Knock / Advanced Ring

The room before the terms.

This is a working decision page for Michael, Caleb, Hudson, Jimmy, Kristin, and Bravetto. Its job is simple: protect the truth of what was built, honor every real contribution, and create options that let the work move without turning the partnership into an IP fight.

Working standard This page is not legal advice, a valuation opinion, or final deal language. It is the clean factual room before counsel turns the shape into paper.

01 / why this room exists

Win-win-win needs a clean center.

Equity cannot carry unclear IP, unclear operating responsibility, and unequal capital contribution all at once.

If we clean this now, we can speak to the market from one center. If we do not, every future deal carries the fracture inside it.

The goal is not to win against each other. The goal is to define what each stream actually contributed, decide what each stream owns, and choose a structure that gives Advanced Knock a real chance to sell without asking Bravetto to pretend its architecture has no value.

The clean move is to separate contribution, ownership, license rights, operating roles, decision rights, and upside. When those are separated, the conversation gets calmer because each question has its own lane.

02 / operating principles

Begin with the end in mind.

The end is a company that can sell honestly, support customers responsibly, and keep the reusable Bravetto substrate intact.

Protect the work.

Bravetto, Michael, and Jimmy built the architecture, code, voice architecture, deployment path, and reusable substrate. That contribution must remain visible.

Honor the market.

Caleb originated the concept and brings live channel opportunity. Hudson contributed the surface and product participation. Those contributions matter.

Move fast cleanly.

The fastest path is not pretending everything is solved. The fastest path is choosing terms simple enough to execute this week.

04 / IP ownership map

What belongs where.

The cleanest structure separates reusable architecture from the market vehicle that sells into one field.

Bravetto-owned

Reusable code, architecture, prompts, voice infrastructure, deployment patterns, data structures, product methods, and the broader substrate that can serve other markets.

Advanced Knock vehicle

The sales vehicle, customer contracts, insurance-specific positioning, operating process, customer relationships, and field-of-use commercial motion.

Third-party rights

John Porter content, Global Life access, partner names, training methods, and any outside materials that require written permission before commercial use.

Core position: Advanced Knock can license and sell the product without owning the Bravetto substrate outright. That protects the architecture and still gives Caleb and Hudson a real business to operate.

If the partners want exclusivity, exclusivity should be priced, limited by field, tied to performance, and reversible if execution stalls.

05 / real options

Three paths, plus the clean exit.

Each path is legitimate. The room should choose one because it is true, not because conflict made everyone tired.

option a

Bravetto product

Bravetto keeps the product, owns the customer relationship, and may compensate Caleb and Hudson for sales, surface contribution, or advisory support.

Best when Bravetto is the operator and the others want upside without carrying company leadership.

option b

Licensed Advanced Knock vehicle

ADVANCEDKNOCK LLC remains the sales vehicle and licenses Bravetto's substrate for a defined field of use. Contribution, operating control, compensation, and ownership are documented cleanly.

This is the likely path of least resistance if Caleb is ready to sell and Bravetto wants to retain architecture rights.

closest fit
option c

90-day pilot license

The partners do not lock permanent ownership yet. They run a short pilot with clear revenue share, support scope, customer rules, and a review date.

Best when trust needs evidence. If pipeline becomes real, permanent terms get easier.

option d

Clean move-on

The partners agree not to operate together. Each side keeps its owned work, third-party rights are respected, and nobody drags the fracture into future deals.

This is not failure. It is the honest path if the operating fit is not there.

06 / license and revenue model

Simple enough to sign.

The model should reward sales execution without converting technical support into an unpaid executive job.

Recommended default: a field-of-use software license from Bravetto to ADVANCEDKNOCK LLC for insurance sales training and related insurance sales enablement.

The license can be non-exclusive at first. If Advanced Knock wants exclusive rights in the insurance vertical, exclusivity should require minimum revenue, minimum customer activity, and a defined term.

Revenue share

Example range: 10% to 20% of gross collected revenue, or 15% to 25% of net revenue after direct platform costs. Counsel and CPA should tune the definitions.

Support retainer

Bravetto support should be scoped. Example: a launch retainer, monthly support fee, or billable engineering bank. No open-ended CTO expectation.

Buyout option

If the vehicle proves revenue, Advanced Knock can negotiate a future buyout, exclusive license, or source escrow. The trigger should be revenue-based, not hope-based.

07 / roles and decision rights

Contribution is not the same as control.

People can be honored for what they brought without giving every person veto power over every decision.

Technical architecture

Bravetto decides what is technically safe, reusable, supportable, and licensable. Advanced Knock can request product changes through a scoped process.

Sales and customers

The operating lead owns pipeline, customer commitments, pilot sequencing, and revenue accountability. If Caleb accepts that role, he needs clear authority and clear reporting.

Product surface

Hudson can own or advise the visible experience if he wants that lane and commits to delivery. Product authority should be tied to actual shipped work.

Company leadership

Someone other than Michael likely needs to lead the Advanced Knock company. Michael and Bravetto can support the software without becoming the management layer.

Bravetto governance

Kristin and Bravetto governance should review how any deal relates to Bravetto, Mataluni Enterprises, capital contribution, taxes, liability, and reusable IP rights.

08 / 90-day pilot path

The quickest clean move.

If the room wants momentum, run a pilot. Do not spend a month arguing over a permanent split before revenue exists.

week 1

Confirm the option, finish launch-critical code, write the short license memo, and identify which John Porter rights require permission.

weeks 2-4

Caleb drives customer meetings and pilot discovery. Bravetto supports only within the agreed launch scope.

days 30/60

Review pipeline, product gaps, support load, customer signal, and actual follow-through. Adjust scope before stress builds.

day 90

Choose permanent license, equity restructure, acquisition, extension, or clean move-on based on evidence.

09 / what we decide together

Do we want to operate together, license the work, or bless the separation?

The next meeting does not need to solve every term. It needs to answer one honest question: which path gives the market, the code, and the people the best chance to move cleanly?
